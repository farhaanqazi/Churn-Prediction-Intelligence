This is your validation instrument — not a portfolio piece. Every design decision serves one purpose: separating browsers from
buyers. Do not add features. Do not make it pretty. Make it produce a dollar figure that surprises the user.
THE OUTPUT (What the user sees)
CHURN AUDIT COMPLETE — 3 Critical Signals Detected

TechFlow Ltd 74/100 $1,800/mo 3 open support tickets
BuilderBase 71/100 $950/mo No login in 18 days
Total Revenue at Risk (next 60 days): $5,150
1 Build the Validator (validator.py)
Days 3–4. Write a Python script that checks an uploaded CSV for: correct column names, no >5% nulls in
critical fields, correct data types. Output: Pass / Warn / Fail report with plain-English fixes. This is the front door
— bad data never reaches the model. python days 3–4 ~3 hrs
2 Build the Scoring Engine (scorer.py)
Days 4–5. Use rule-based logic only — NOT XGBoost at this stage. Three rules: (1) Login count dropped >50%
in 30 days = High Risk. (2) More than 2 open support tickets = Medium Risk. (3) No payment in 45 days = High
Risk. Assign 0–100 score. Attach MRR to each customer row. python scikit-learn days 4–5 ~4 hrs
3 Build the Streamlit App (app.py)
Days 5–7. Single page. One CSV upload button. Output: the ranked risk table with MRR at Risk column. One
bold total: 'Total Revenue at Risk: $X'. One upgrade prompt below the table. Two buttons: 'Yes, show me the
full OS' (captures email) and 'No thanks' (logs dismissal to a Google Sheet via a free API call). streamlit days 5–7 ~5 hrs
4 Build the Auto PDF Report (report.py)
Days 7–8. When a user clicks 'Yes, show me', generate a PDF using reportlab. Header: 'Churn Intelligence
Audit — [Company] — [Date]'. Body: the risk table, the total revenue at risk, and one paragraph of plain-English
interpretation. This PDF is emailed to them. It will be forwarded. Every forward is free distribution. reportlab days 7–8 ~4 hrs

THE DELIVERY PROCESS — 10 STEPS
D1 Send Intake Form
Share a Notion form (free) collecting: number of active customers, CSV export from their CRM, primary CSM
contact name, billing period. Set 48-hour turnaround expectation.
D2 Run the Validator
Run their CSV through validator.py. Email results within 4 hours. If data fails, send a plain-English fix list. Do
not proceed until they pass the validator — this protects both sides.
D3 Build Bronze Layer
Load their raw CSV into DuckDB as the Bronze table. Append ingested_at timestamp. Run idempotency
check — ensure no duplicate rows.
D4 Build Silver Layer
Standardise column names to your canonical schema. Resolve any identity mismatches. Flag and drop any
records that cannot be linked across data sources.
D5 Build Gold Layer (Feature Store)
Calculate the 5 standard features: logins_30d, logins_prev_30d, usage_drop_30d, support_tickets_7d,
days_since_last_payment. This is what the scoring engine reads.
D6 Run the Scoring Engine
Apply the rule-based scorer to every customer. Assign risk score (0–100) and primary_risk_factor label.
Export as enriched_customers.csv.

5 Domain Specialisation & Scale
Month 3–6 · Moat-building
This is where your NHS and banking background becomes an uncopiable competitive advantage. Generic churn tools break
when applied to regulated industries. You know exactly why — and you know how to fix it. No one else in this market has that
combination.
WHY GENERIC CHURN MODELS FAIL IN YOUR DOMAINS
Signal Generic SaaS Model Says Your Domain Knowledge Says
Low login frequency High churn risk Healthcare: product is working autonomously. Cybersecurity: system is healthy.
High support tickets Friction ® churn risk Enterprise: engaged client investing in implementation ® retention signal
Sudden ticket drop Problem resolved Banking: client gave up trying and is quietly evaluating alternatives
Flat MRR Stable account Fintech: volume may be migrating to competitor while contract stays active
Annual contract renewal Standard event NHS: 18-month lead time on exits. The signal is 14 months before renewal, not 30 days.
YOUR TWO DOMAIN VARIANTS
Healthcare / NHS Variant
• Signals: clinical adoption rate, admin vs clinical login ratio, implementation completion %, IT ticket taxonomy
• Model: Survival Analysis (Cox regression) — churn is rare, lead times are long, XGBoost will overfit
• Compliance: NHS IG Toolkit validator, DSPT alignment check, local DuckDB (no cloud — more compliant)
• Output format: NHS Board Paper style — specific structure, specific language, RAG status by directorate
• Intervention playbook: Multi-stakeholder QBR — clinical lead + IT director + finance simultaneously
• Your moat: You worked inside Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust. You know System One quirks, PAS export
formats, and the exact language a Trust Board Chair expects on slide 2.
Fintech / Banking Variant
• Signals: transaction volume trend, API error rate delta, support ticket taxonomy, competitor mention frequency
• Model: Gradient boosting + volume migration detector — partial churn (revenue leaves before logo leaves)
• Compliance: FCA SS1/23 model validation framework, PCI DSS field checker, GDPR purpose limitation validator
• Output format: Risk Committee Pack — RAG status, SLA performance trend, model confidence interval
• Intervention playbook: Formal Service Review with documented SLA performance — not an informal catch-up
• Your moat: You operated inside J&K; Bank. You know what a risk committee asks for, what a relationship manager
needs, and why those are three different documents from the same data.
